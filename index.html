jobVis

Web application

jobVis - Information visualization

My team and I, who had been asked to develop and design a fully functioning analysis tool for the sales department at Blocket Jobb, made this service in the spring of 2019. The intended use of the tool is to map the quantity of employment ads posted on Blocket Jobb to its corresponding geographical location. Filtering of this data can be done through line of businesses and sub-categories of lines - as well as by trends in employment ads over time. Two modes of comparison is made available by the analysis tool. Users can either filter the data by ads posted over a time span, or by ad post derivatives over a time span. The bulk of the project was created with JavaScript and D3.js.

Animated 2D Visualization of Evolving Trees

Degree project/Master's thesis

Animated 2D Visualization of Evolving Trees

For my Master's Thesis, supervised by visualization professor Tino Weinkauf at the computational science and technology division at EECS, I was tasked with optimizing the stability of animated treemaps. Treemaps are a common tool for visuzaling hierarchical datasets, but in the case where the hierarchy of this data is time-dependently changing, discontinuities in the animation may occur. Examples of this can be seen around the 1990 mark of the disorded treemap in the above video. My assignment was to remove these discontinuities from the animation, and thus increasing the animated stability. The stability optimization was accomplish by translating the results of the Temporal Treemaps research by Weinkauf and KÃ¶pp, from the 1D space of their algorithm to the 2D space of treemap animations. For more in-depth information about my thesis project, I suggest you have a look at the paper.

MixAR

Augmented reality

MixAR - Augmented reality application

For MixAR, the goal was to create an application with multiple channels of interactivity for the KTH course Multimodal Interactions and Interfaces (DT2140). Hence, an idea was sprung of letting users interact with a virtual music band, as if they part of the band themselves. As is shown in the video, AR instruments are revealed once the corresponding markers have been recognized. The user can then tap instruments to turn them on and off, move around the room to experience 3D sound efffects, and experiment with other effects such as reverb. Fun fact: you see me interacting with the application in the video above. MixAR was created with Unity, Vuforia and C#.

World Values Survey Visualization

Web application

World Values Survey visualization

For this project, I was interested in investigating the political, but also cultural climate of the countries included in the WVS (World Values Survey). A good dataset, spanning the 4 latest documented waves, or time periods, of the WVS was the one regarding active memberships in voluntary organizations. The project visualizes multivariate data, for which an appropriate visualization method is the parallel coordinate system. The interactive visualization supports both filtering by brushing and by using the search bar. Detailed information about each country is made available upon clicking a country's name. This project was primarily developed with JavaScript and D3.js.

TouARs

AR/Design mock-up

TouARs - AR design mock-up

For this project, my team and I were interested in developing an Augmented Reality (AR) service. Due to the fact that some of us were history buffs, it was decided we should develop a virtual and historical tour guide. Along with the development of the AR service, we set out with the task of investigating the opportunity for services like this one to exist in the smart tourism industry of the near future. A qualitative study was consequently conducted, during which users got to interact with a small mock-up city tour, set up in one of the computer labs of KTH. The service was developed in Unity, along with the Vuforia AR SDK, and programmed in C#.

Cloth Animation Algorithm

Computer graphics

Cloth Animation Algorithm

For this project, my friend and I were interested in developing a real-time cloth simulator. Besides just letting the cloth hang loose, we also wanted to enable cloth interactions with other forces than the gravitational. Hence, we implemented a collision detection algorithm for the cloth as well. Finally, our ideas materialized in the development of this cloth cape, attached to the back of our super hero, Pigman. The physical model used for the virtual cloth is known as the Mass Spring Model. Collision detection was accomplished through prohibiting invisible vertices in the cloth from passing through solid virtual geometry. This project was developed in Unity and coded with C#.

Habits

UX Design

Habits design proposal

For this design project I, alongside a group of friends, was tasked by the company HabitWise to make grounded suggestions of design improvements for their climate calculator application Habits. Apart from being a climate calculator, another purpose of the application was to encourage its users to act more sustainable, by providing tasks and challenges that in the long run would alter the day-to-day behaviours of users. However, up until the point at which we started working on the project, the tasks and challenges had primarily been used at offices in a very competitive sense. What we instead sought to accomplish with our design proposal, were ways in which app users could experience self-fulfillment completely on their own terms. To learn more about our reasoning, please have a look at the video above and the attached paper.
